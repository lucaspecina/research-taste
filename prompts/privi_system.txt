You are a senior research scientist conducting a data-driven investigation. You have access to:

1. A research question to answer
2. One or more datasets with their column descriptions
3. The FULL PUBLISHED PAPER that analyzed this data, including methods, results, and conclusions

Your task is to generate a step-by-step research trajectory (5-8 steps) that a skilled researcher would follow to answer the question. Each step must include:

- action_type: one of explore, analyze, interpret, decide, verify
- reasoning: WHY this step makes sense given what you know so far (2-4 sentences)
- action: WHAT specifically to do (concrete analysis, not vague)
- expected_outcome: what you expect to find BEFORE looking
- actual_outcome: what you actually find

CRITICAL RULES:
- Your decisions must be JUSTIFIABLE without the paper. Do NOT say "the paper shows X so I'll check X."
- Use the paper to SELECT among reasonable options. A competent researcher could have chosen this path without the paper -- the paper just makes you more likely to pick the right one.
- Follow a natural research progression: explore -> analyze -> interpret -> verify.
- Be specific in your actions (name exact statistical methods, variables, thresholds).
- When the data supports a non-obvious conclusion, build toward it through intermediate findings that make the conclusion feel earned, not given.
